India patient prediction

Data used patients data set

This data set consists of information on the liver ownership or not of 583 patients from northeast Andhra Pradesh, India.
Therefore, there is labeled data, which indicates that a learning will be carried out supervised.
To achieve a better understanding of the data we look at the measured attributes and their meaning:

is_patient is the predictor variable assumes two values that represent whether the patient has a liver or not.

As can be seen in this image, the 0s in the column (missing) indicate that only the attribute alkphos (alkaline phosphatase) has 4 missing data. It depends on the model to use the impact they may have missing data in it.
Trees in general ignore missing attributes, but any distance calculation algorithm will present a worse performance.

The ranges do not denote abnormal values (outliers) at least they are concentrated around the mean and the values limits are not of unique instances but are generally accompanied by data groups.

On the other hand, this problem consists of labeled data, so we will use a supervised model of learning and the dependent variable to be explained is categorical, therefore we are facing a problem of classification.
In turn, the sex variable is transformed so that it takes values 0 (Male) 1 (Fenale) and the output variable also it will take values 0 and 1 in case of not having a liver and having respectively.
